Vegetable Manchurian

A crispy, tangy Indo-Chinese appetizer with vegetable balls in a flavorful sauce.

Ingredients

For the Manchurian Balls:

  • 1 cup finely chopped cabbage
  • 1/2 cup grated carrots
  • 1/4 cup chopped onions
  • 1/4 cup chopped bell peppers
  • 2 tbsp all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 2 tbsp cornstarch
  • 1 tsp ginger-garlic paste
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Oil for frying

For the Sauce:

  • 1 tbsp oil
  • 1 tbsp finely chopped garlic
  • 1 tbsp finely chopped ginger
  • 1/4 cup chopped onions
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp chili sauce
  • 1 tbsp tomato ketchup
  • 1 tsp vinegar
  • 1 cup water or vegetable stock
  • 1 tbsp cornstarch mixed with 2 tbsp water (for thickening)
  • Spring onions for garnish

Instructions

  1. Mix all ball ingredients except oil. Form into small balls and deep-fry until golden. Drain excess oil.
  2. In a pan, heat oil and saut√© garlic, ginger, and onions until fragrant.
  3. Add soy sauce, chili sauce, ketchup, and vinegar. Stir well.
  4. Add water or stock, bring to a boil, then stir in cornstarch slurry to thicken the sauce.
  5. Add fried balls to the sauce, toss to coat, and cook for 2-3 minutes.
  6. Garnish with spring onions and serve hot as an appetizer.

Enjoy your crispy Vegetable Manchurian! Serves 4 as an appetizer.
